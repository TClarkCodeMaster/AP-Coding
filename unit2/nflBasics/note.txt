        
            Asking Good Questions
    Clarity- Question is easy to understand
    Focused- The question is direct and not too broad.
    Measurable- Question can be answered/proven using data.
    Insightful- Provide new information. (It should trigger a new idea/question.)
            How Not To Ask Questions
    Vague- Dont ask questions that aren't clear.
    Unmeasurable- Don't ask questions that can't be measured/analyzed using statistics.
    Recognize Incompleteness- Don't mistake a cause with a pattern without sufficient data to back it up.
    Complexity- Don't make your question too complex. keep It Simple.
            Types Of Research Questions
    Descriptive- Summarize what happened.
    Comparative- Compare groups.
